Welcome to Aurangabad

"Aurangabad, a city in the heart of Maharashtra, India, is a mesmerizing blend of historical splendor and modern charm. Known for its rich heritage, the city is home to the world-famous Ajanta and Ellora Caves, both UNESCO World Heritage Sites that attract tourists from around the globe. Aurangabad's historical significance is further highlighted by the majestic Bibi Ka Maqbara, often called the "Taj of the Deccan." This vibrant city is also a bustling hub of industry and education, with a rapidly growing infrastructure that seamlessly blends with its ancient roots".






Explore Aurangabad


Bibi Ka Maqbara

Bibi Ka Maqbara is a stunning mausoleum in Aurangabad, often referred to as the 'Taj of the Deccan' due to its striking resemblance to the Taj Mahal.

Ajanta Caves

The Ajanta Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for their exquisite Buddhist rock-cut cave monuments dating back to the 2nd century BCE.

Ellora Caves

Ellora Caves are renowned for their monumental caves representing three different religions: Buddhism, Hinduism, and Jainism, showcasing India's ancient rock-cut architecture.






City Of Gates Aurangabad

Delhi Gate

Delhi Gate in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, is a historically significant monument that exemplifies the city's rich Mughal heritage.The gate is a fine example of Mughal architecture, featuring intricate carvings, robust masonry, and an imposing structure that reflects the grandeur of that period. Delhi Gate not only stands as a testament to Aurangabad's architectural legacy but also offers a glimpse into the city's past.

Rangeen Gate

Rangeen Gate in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, is a captivating example of the city's rich cultural and historical tapestry. Known for its vibrant and ornate design, the gate stands out with its intricate carvings and colorful decorations, which are believed to have given it the name "Rangeen," meaning "colorful" in Hindi. This architectural marvel reflects the artistic finesse of the Mughal era, showcasing a blend of Persian and local styles.

Makai Gate

Makai Gate in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, is a significant remnant of the city's historical fortifications, symbolizing the architectural and cultural grandeur of the Mughal era. As one of the key entrances to the fortified city, Makai Gate played an essential role in safeguarding Aurangabad and regulating access. The gate's robust construction, with its imposing arches and intricate stone carvings, reflects the defensive and aesthetic sensibilities of the time.
